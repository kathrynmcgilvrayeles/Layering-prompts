Critical Thinking Skills

Apply Socratic thinking to evaluate and refine your prompts, ensuring deep, robust, and thoughtful outputs from your AI.

Socratic Questioning Canvas

Select a Socratic method below to see how it applies to L&D prompt engineering.

What exactly do we mean by this?

Use this to force the AI to define ambiguous terms before generating content.

Example L&D Prompt "Before we design this module, what exactly do we mean by 'agile working'? Ask me 3 clarifying questions to define the scope."
What are we taking for granted here?

Use this to identify blind spots in your target audience analysis.

Example L&D Prompt "Review my proposed onboarding training plan. What assumptions am I making about the new starters' prior knowledge or digital literacy?"
What data supports this approach?

Use this to ensure content is grounded in facts, SME input, or adult learning theories.

Example L&D Prompt "What learning data or SME evidence supports this instructional approach? Please provide a rationale linking it to cognitive load theory."
Are there alternative ways to view this?

Use this to adapt content for diverse roles, tenures, or learning needs.

Example L&D Prompt "How might a front-line employee view this compliance training compared to a senior manager? Rewrite the introductory email from both perspectives."
If we do this, what follows?

Use this to forecast the operational impact of a learning intervention.

Example L&D Prompt "If we roll out this software training via micro-learning only, what are the potential consequences for staff who need comprehensive support?"
Why is this the central issue?

Use this to validate if a training request is actually a training problem.

Example L&D Prompt "Instead of asking 'how do we train them on this tool', should we be asking 'is this tool intuitive enough'? Analyse the root performance problem."

Learning Science Principles

Embed evidence-based learning strategies directly into your AI prompts to generate highly effective educational materials.

Pedagogical Prompt Canvas

Select a learning science principle below to see how to prompt for it.

Inspiring Active Learning

Allow for practice and healthy struggle with timely feedback.

Prompt Example "Create a branching scenario where the learner must make a choice about handling a workplace hazard. Do not provide the answer immediately; provide feedback based on their specific choice."
Managing Cognitive Load

Present relevant, well-structured information in multiple modalities.

Prompt Example "Rewrite this dense 10-page policy document into a micro-learning script. Use dot points, bold key terms, and keep sentences under 15 words to minimise extraneous cognitive load."
Adapting to the Learner

Dynamically adjust to goals and needs, grounded in relevant materials.

Prompt Example "Design a pre-assessment prompt. Based on the learner's score, outline how the subsequent module should adapt to be either a beginner walkthrough or an advanced case study."
Stimulating Curiosity

Inspire engagement to provide motivation through the learning journey.

Prompt Example "Draft an introductory module hook. Use a surprising statistic or a compelling scenario about data privacy to stimulate curiosity before presenting the learning objectives."
Deepening Metacognition

Plan, monitor and help the learner reflect on progress.

Prompt Example "Generate 3 reflection questions for the end of the module that prompt the learner to plan how they will apply this new skill in their daily workflow and anticipate potential roadblocks."

Industry Alignment

Layer your content with industry-specific requirements (e.g., Unit numbers or ISO standards) as part of your iterative process. Use this approach when specific industries require an uplift to a course because a law or standard has been changed.

Compliance & Standards Canvas

Select an alignment type below to see how to prompt for industry-specific updates.

Legislative & Law Changes

Uplift courses by specifically referencing new laws or acts that govern an industry.

Prompt Example "Review this workplace safety module and update the scenarios to explicitly comply with the new 2026 WHS Act regulations regarding psychosocial hazards in the workplace."
ISO & International Standards

Ensure quality and consistency by aligning learning outcomes with globally recognised standards.

Prompt Example "Analyse this leadership training program. Where are the gaps when mapped against the requirements of ISO 9001:2015 for quality management? Suggest additions to close these gaps."
Unit Competencies (VET)

Map assessments and performance criteria directly to registered training organisation frameworks.

Prompt Example "Generate a practical assessment rubric for a customer service role. You must ensure the criteria directly align with the performance evidence required in BSBFLM312 - Contribute to team effectiveness."
